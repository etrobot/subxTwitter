Concerns about Open Sourcing AI

14 Jan 2024 20:36:41
Author: @rao2z
Twitter Link: Full Tweet

But won't they misuse 'em, if we open source? #SundayHarangue
Some folks tend to stress about the "what if it falls into the wrong hands?" aspect of open source #AI. But for every such worry, there are the oh so many "imagine when it falls into the right hands!" reasons to be sanguine.
Open sourcing a model--instead of licensing access--is lot like teaching people to fish vs. giving them (right to buy) fish.

Open Source R&D

Quoting: Subbarao Kambhampati (‡∞ï‡∞Ç‡∞≠‡∞Ç‡∞™‡∞æ‡∞ü‡∞ø ‡∞∏‡±Å‡∞¨‡±ç‡∞¨‡∞æ‡∞∞‡∞æ‡∞µ‡±Å) (@rao2z)
The debate on AI risks and need for legislation is a complex one and my own position is not exactly identical to anything any of the key players have already publicized. I am fully supportive of open-source R&D--something @ylecun has admirably carried the torch since the beginning.
I do believe that there is a critical need for legislative and societal oversight on the deployment of #AI systems.
But, any discussion on #AI deployment risks often seems to be hijacked by the hypothetical existential risk arguments that suck the oxygen right out of the room.
