Samantha LaDuc (@SamanthaLaDuc): "Serious question:

Why is Bitcoin “squirreled away & doesn't trade”?

A CURRENCY is by definition a means of exchange, and that is in active circulation.

A COMMODITY is by definition a raw material that can be bought and sold, and that is in active circulation.

An ASSET is by definition a useful or valuable thing, that can be bought and sold, with circulation dependent on whether it is liquid or illiquid.

And all of the above can be either popular or rare, in a state of supply-demand balance, but the quantity/quality of TRANSACTIONS determines its relative value.

Bitcoin is unique.

Since Bitcoin is most certainly not a currency nor commodity nor asset enabling TRANSACTIONS, it is therefore an illiquid asset that has no utility or value beyond speculation that it will be a liquid asset someday in the future, or a plaything for those who manipulate the emotions of those who believe it will be.

So what is needing to happen exactly for Bitcoin to have real intrinsic value? To make it useful. To conduct transactions with it. Tell me! I’m really curious!

But until then, why hoard bitcoin if the premium that is baked into its price reflects this future utopian world of a utility product in circulation as means of exchange to serve as replacement to currently-deemed inferior currencies and commodities?

Another words: given the scarcity of actual value now, why hoard a future depreciating, illiquid asset that serves no purpose other than speculation on price appreciation from extrinsically assigned value?

Trading it (and it’s highly levered proxies) makes a lot of sense to me - in both directions ! ! Even holding it until just before the mania ends, I can understand!
