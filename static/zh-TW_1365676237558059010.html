11 Jan 2024 13:30:23 @dickiebush: A powerful identity that has served me well:
“I will make any investment in myself have a positive return.”
• Courses
• Employees
• Masterminds
• Personal chef
• 1:1 mentorship
• Paid advertising
• Upgraded hotels
• Top-tier work setup
• Business class flights
• Memorable experiences
I’ve started to allocate more and more money to these areas that, in the past, I would have put into “traditional investments” like stocks/crypto.
Now, it took some internal work to get here.
Growing up, I had a troubled scarcity mindset with money.
I was the poorest kid at my school, saw rich people as evil, and thought the only way to increase my bank account balance was to live a frugal lifestyle and save, save, save.
So the first time I bought each of these, it was uncomfortable.
For example, 3 years ago I bought my first $150 course.
I was so terrified of spending that money that I called my friend and asked him to split it with me and I would share the logins.
But that initial investment has yielded (literally) a 100000% return.
And that gave me a bit of evidence that it was worth it to make investments in myself.
Now, that feeling of discomfort didn’t go away with one piece of evidence—it returned the first time I made any new investment.
When I brought on my first employee, I was terrified at how much I was paying them.
When I upgraded my hotel for the first time, I was terrified at how much it cost.
When I paid $68,000 to join a mastermind, I woke up in the middle of the night thinking it was a giant mistake.
And on and on and on.
But each of these investments in myself brought out a new level of effort, intensity, and efficiency.
And that naturally leads to increased earning potential.
So now, I have a fat stack of evidence that every time I make an investment in myself, I put in the effort necessary to make it have a positive return.
And the best part?
Anyone can make the decision today to adopt this mindset.
And chances are, you have a stack of evidence from former investments in yourself that led to positive returns—you just haven’t looked closely.
And if you don’t feel like you have this list of evidence, you either:
• Haven’t made investments in yourself
• Didn’t enter into your former investments with the mindset that you will do whatever is necessary to make it a positive return
Both of those can change today.
Now I don’t want to exaggerate here, but I feel like this mindset makes me a superhuman.
Everywhere I look I see an opportunity to invest in myself.
And with every investment in myself, I become better and better, which leads to more resources to reinvest in myself, and on and on this flywheel spins.
